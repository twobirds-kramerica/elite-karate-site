## Findings

### F1 — Contact section missing from navigation (P2)

**System:** Navigation
**Principle violated:** Dan Brown #4 Front Doors — every page should be navigable from wherever a user lands
**Observation:** Nav includes 6 items: Home, Our Story, Classes, Dojo Floor, Schedule, Find Us. The `#contact` section (the primary conversion point with the "Book a Free Class" email CTA) has no nav entry. "Find Us" links to the map section below contact.
**Problem:** A parent scanning the nav for how to reach the dojo finds "Find Us" (address + map) but no "Contact" or "Book" entry. Direct navigation to contact requires either scrolling past 5 sections or clicking a CTA that only appears after reading the hero. Users who return to book a class cannot jump directly there.
**Fix direction:** Add "Contact" or "Book Free Class" nav item linking to `#contact`. Desktop and mobile nav. The item should appear after "Schedule" since that's the logical decision path (learn schedule → book a class).
**Effort estimate:** Low
**Implemented:** Yes — see index.html (desktop nav line ~33, mobile nav line ~47)

---

### F2 — "Dojo Floor" nav label is brand-specific, not wayfinding-standard (P3)

**System:** Labelling
**Principle violated:** Dan Brown #1 Objects — users should be able to predict destination from label
**Observation:** The gallery/photos section is labelled "Dojo Floor" in the nav. The section heading is "The Dojo Floor."
**Problem:** Parents and adults looking for photos of the club or facility would scan for "Gallery," "Photos," or "Our Club" — not "Dojo Floor." The label is evocative brand language but reduces predictability for first-time visitors.
**Fix direction:** Options: (a) rename to "Gallery" for clarity, (b) keep "Dojo Floor" as intentional brand language and accept the trade-off, (c) test with real users. Recommend Aaron decide — this is a brand-vs-findability judgment call, not a clear structural error.
**Effort estimate:** Low (if Aaron decides to rename)
**Implemented:** No — requires Aaron's brand decision

---

### F3 — Nav label / section heading inconsistency (P3)

**System:** Labelling
**Principle violated:** Consistency principle (Rosenfeld/Morville) — same concept should carry same name throughout
**Observation:**
- Nav "Classes" → `<h2>` "Our Classes"
- Nav "Schedule" → `<h2>` "Class Schedule"
- Nav "Dojo Floor" → `<h2>` "The Dojo Floor"
- Nav "Home" → `<h1>` "Modern Tradition." (brand tagline, not a label match)
**Problem:** Minor inconsistency. In a single-page site this is low-friction, but it creates a small mismatch if a screen reader user announces the heading they've landed on vs what the nav link said.
**Fix direction:** Align nav labels with `<h2>` text, or vice versa. Lowest-effort: update nav labels to "Our Classes," "Class Schedule" — but this makes the nav slightly wordier. Alternatively, update `<h2>` to match the shorter nav labels.
**Effort estimate:** Low
**Implemented:** No — defer to Phase 2 design review; current nav is concise and functional
