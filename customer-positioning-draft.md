## Who Actually Shows Up

**Primary: The Cautious Parent**
- Parent of a child 5–12 in St. Thomas or Elgin County
- Looking for a structured after-school activity with discipline payoff
- The question in their head: *"Will this be good for my kid, or is it just fighting?"*
- Research path: Google "karate for kids St. Thomas" → reads Google reviews → lands on site → checks schedule + price → if no friction, books a free class
- Biggest friction: commitment language ("Enrol Now", "Join the Team") reads as contract obligation
- Key trust signal: photos of real kids + real instructor, not stock art

**Secondary: The Adult Seeker**
- Adult 25–45, sedentary, wants fitness that doesn't feel like a gym
- May have martial arts interest from childhood
- Self-motivated — will show up without much hand-holding if the schedule works
- Trust signal: instructor credentials + community feel

**Tertiary: Community referral**
- Word of mouth via school, church, rec league
- Already partially sold — needs schedule + location confirmation, not conversion

## What They Actually Decide On

Conversion research (Monstro, Veuze Media, Clubworx, 2025):
- Conversion rate with "free trial class" CTA: 50–60% to membership
- Conversion rate with generic "Contact Us" / "Enrol Now": below 20%
- Decision is made in under 90 seconds on mobile — schedule visibility matters more than copy

The gap between the current site and a high-converting site is almost entirely the CTA ("Join the Team" → "Book a Free Class") and the absence of testimonials.

## Pricing Reality Check

Current site: no pricing information. This is the norm for martial arts schools (they use price on enquiry to filter leads), but research shows it increases bounce rate among parent audiences who want to self-qualify before contacting.

**Recommended approach for Phase 2:**
Add a single line in the FAQ or contact section: *"Monthly membership fees start at $[X]/month. Contact us for family rates and beginner packages."*

Aaron/Kirk to supply the actual figure. If Kirk is not comfortable publishing prices, the alternative is: *"Training fees are membership-based. Enquire for current rates — no contracts, no surprises."*

## Tone + Voice Guidelines

**Do:**
- Confidence without boast: "Our students earn their belts" (not "we produce champions")
- Welcoming specificity: "Ages 4 and up, no experience required" (not "all ages welcome")
- Community roots: "St. Thomas, Ontario" named explicitly and often
- Real names: "Sensei Trevor Kirk" (not "our head instructor")

**Avoid:**
- Generic martial arts tropes: "discipline of mind, body and soul"
- Pressure language: "limited spots", "enrol today before it's full"
- Vague credentials: "certified instructor" without specifics
- Americanisms: "defense" → "defence", "honor" → "honour"
